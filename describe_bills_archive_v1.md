## Laken Riley Act (S.5)

Named after a Georgia nursing student murdered in 2024, this bill requires federal immigration authorities to detain undocumented immigrants who are charged with or arrested for theft-related crimes — including shoplifting, burglary, and larceny — as well as assault of a law enforcement officer or any crime causing death or serious bodily injury. Detention is mandatory even before a conviction. The bill also gives state attorneys general the legal authority to sue federal immigration officials in court if they believe federal enforcement decisions — such as releasing someone who should have been detained — have harmed their state. The Congressional Budget Office estimated full implementation could cost roughly $83 billion over three years, though the bill provides no new funding.
